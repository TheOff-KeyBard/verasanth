## NEW §A.7 — The Dak'Aridari: Full Canon

### A.7.1 Origin

The Dak'Aridari were not born underground.
They were left there.

In the early cycles of Verasanth, the demon
dragged captives across the veil to populate
a city it had been compelled to complete.
Some were deposited too deep. Into the lower
strata — foundation tunnels, vaults, and
echoing stone chambers that predated the
demon's arrival.

The demon assumed they died.

The city kept them.

And reshaped them.

### A.7.2 The Descent

The lower strata were not simply dark.
They were unstable. When the Pale Fires
awakened and the city began to shift, the
lower layer experienced a structural collapse.
Walls moved. Floors folded. Light vanished.

A portion of the early population was trapped.

They adapted or died.

Those who adapted became the Dak'Aridari.

**The Dak'Aridari are not creatures of darkness.
They are what remains when the city forgets
to provide light.**

### A.7.3 What the Lower Strata Did to Them

The lower strata are not truly dark. They are
lit by bioluminescent fungal webs, faint
echo-lights that shimmer when sound bounces,
and mineral veins that glow under pressure.

Their eyes evolved — or were reshaped — to read:
- Vibration
- Heat gradients
- Ultraviolet reflections
- The emotional echo of stone

They don't see darkness. They see depth.

### A.7.4 The Two-Phase Truth

**The Dak'Aridari were not always hunted.
Once, they were studied. Once, they were returned.
Now, they are taken. And they do not come back.**

---

**PHASE 1 — The Silent Collection**

The demon did not understand what the
Dak'Aridari had become. It observed.
Clinical. Distant. Almost respectful.

People disappeared without struggle.
No destruction. No fire. No chaos.
Some returned — changed, quiet, distant.

Elders remember this era:
*"They didn't hurt you. They just took you
somewhere you couldn't remember."*

The demon was studying specimens. It did not
yet know what it needed them for.

---

**PHASE 2 — The Burning Hunt**

The demon eventually understood.

The Dak'Aridari can navigate the lower strata —
regions of the city beyond the demon's reach.
Regions that predate its involvement entirely.
Regions that belong to the same ancient force
that birthed the Ledger.

Once the demon grasped this, its attitude
shifted sharply. It realized:
- It accidentally created them
- They evolved beyond its control
- They can access the city's forbidden layers
- They are essential to its search
- And they owe it nothing

This infuriates the demon. It cannot negotiate.
It cannot compel. It cannot descend.

So it resorts to force.

The kidnappings became violent. Targeted.
Desperate. Personal.

Younger Dak'Aridari know only this era:
*"They stopped asking. Now they take."*

---

**The generational fracture this creates:**

Elders: *"They were not cruel. You could survive it."*
Youth: *"Run if you see it. There is no surviving now."*

This conflicting testimony is immediate
dialogue fuel. Both accounts are true.
The demon changed. The threat evolved.

### A.7.5 The Demon's Emotional Core

The Dak'Aridari are the only thing the demon
needs that it cannot control — that it
accidentally created.

That creates: **resentment + dependence + urgency.**

The demon resents them because:
- They are its mistake
- They are its limitation
- They are its only hope

### A.7.6 What the Ledger Says About Them

Unlike the Ashborn, the Ledger can record the
Dak'Aridari. It doesn't like what it sees.

It needs them.

Ledger entries (flavor text, not literal UI):

*"Dak'Aridari: subsurface adaptation detected.
Origin: displacement event.
Note: subject navigates restricted strata
with anomalous ease."*

*"Warning: lower-city readings unstable.
Correction applied.
Source: Dak'Aridari presence.
Recommend continued observation."*

**Contrast with Ashborn:**
- Ashborn: Ledger cannot record them (anomaly)
- Dak'Aridari: Ledger records them and needs them (dependency)

### A.7.7 Geography — The Depth Layers

```
City Surface
    ↓
Sewers (structured decay, Act 1)
    ↓
Foundation / Early Deep (pressure + mystery)
    ↓
The Scar (transitional — unstable, biological)
    ↓
Lower Strata (Deep-Echo / Hollow-Deep / Unspoken)
```

The Scar is the threshold where the city stops
pretending to be stable. It connects to the
Lower Strata but they are distinct regions.
The Scar is organic and reactive. The Lower
Strata are where the city's original
architecture still functions — or has become
something else entirely.

Flavor for entering the Scar:
*"The stone changes here. It doesn't echo the
same way. Something beneath it is listening back."*

### A.7.8 The Three Tiers of the Deep

These are world content — not player choices.
**Never name these tiers in player-facing text.**
Let players discover the pattern and name it
themselves.

---

**Tier 1 — The Deep-Echo (Hidden Faction)**

*"They endured — and adapted with intention."*

Found stable pockets in the lower strata.
Still Dak'Aridari. Hyper-attuned to stone
memory. Almost perfectly silent. Ritualistic,
deliberate, deeply territorial.

Not monstrous. Not safe. Ancient and precise.

Player experience:
*"They knew you were there before you stepped
into the room."*

Role: Guides to deeper areas. Gatekeepers.
Philosophical mirror for Dak'Aridari players.
Potential allies — not easily earned.

---

**Tier 2 — The Hollow-Deep (Enemies)**

*"They endured — and were overwritten."*

Fell into unstable, fractured zones where the
city's memory breaks. Echo-warped hunters.
Glitch-like silhouettes. Movements that don't
quite align with reality. Fragments of identity
looping endlessly.

Tragic. Unsettling. Almost recognizable.

Player experience:
*"It pauses. Like it remembers how to be still.
Then it forgets again."*

Role: Core undercity enemies. Horror encounters.
Emotional gut-punch for Dak'Aridari players —
this is what they could have become.

---

**Tier 3 — The Unspoken (Endgame Mystery)**

*"They endured — and became something the city
refuses to define."*

The Ledger does not record them.

Entry attempt failed. No stable form detected.
Do not proceed.

Not enemies in a conventional sense.
Not a faction. Not fully beings.
Something fused with the city's original
architecture. Living nodes of city memory.
The true interface between the city and
whatever built it.

**Do not fully define these yet.**
Plant the hook. Let players find the edge of it.

Role: Mythic endgame layer. The demon cannot
reach them. The Ledger cannot define them.
Something the demon fears more than the
Dak'Aridari themselves.

### A.7.9 The Three Player Sub-types

Set during character creation if race === "dakaridari".
Flag: dak_adaptation

These are adaptation paths, not classes.
They should feel like how your body survived
the undercity — not a specialization you
trained into.

**Design layer:**
- Race = what the city did to you
- Sub-type = how you adapted to survive
- Instinct = who you became after

---

**The Echo-Sensed**
*"The stone speaks. You listen."*

Developed heightened perception — evolutionary,
not magical. Reads micro-vibrations, airflow
shifts, emotional resonance in stone, faint
ultraviolet reflections. The scouts, the
interpreters. Can tell when a tunnel is about
to shift.

Traits: Hyper-awareness. Uncanny stillness.
Listening posture. Eyes that catch faint
violet glints.

City reaction: The walls subtly align around
them, as if making room.

Flavor: *"You hear the space before you see it."*

Flag value: "echo_sensed"

---

**The Veil-Walked**
*"Shadows are not absence. They are passage."*

Adapted to the undercity's fractured light.
Move through shadow the way others move
through crowds — slipping, bending, vanishing
at the edges of perception. Not teleportation.
Not magic. Perfect exploitation of darkness.

Traits: Silent movement. Uncanny timing.
Ability to disappear by stepping into the
right angle of shadow. A reputation for being
where they shouldn't be.

City reaction: Lights dim slightly when they
pass, as if the city is helping them hide.

Flavor: *"You are not where you were a moment ago."*

Flag value: "veil_walked"

---

**The Stone-Blooded**
*"Pressure shapes all things. You simply endured."*

Survived the deepest, most crushing parts of
the undercity. Bodies adapted: denser muscle,
shock absorption, resistance to pressure and
echo-distortion. Calm, grounded presence.
The anchors of Dak'Aridari society.

Traits: Slow, deliberate movement. Low,
resonant voices. A sense of physical and
emotional gravity.

City reaction: The stone quiets around them,
as if recognizing kin.

Flavor: *"The weight does not move you."*

Flag value: "stone_blooded"

### A.7.11 Cultural Identity

Three core principles of Dak'Aridari culture:

1. **Silence is information.**
   If someone isn't speaking, that means something.

2. **Movement is risk.**
   You move only when you understand the space.

3. **Secrets are currency.**
   Information is more valuable than gold.

Cultural behaviors:
- Avoid unnecessary noise instinctively
- Conversations include long pauses
- Rarely answer directly the first time
- Prefer indirect paths — physically and socially

### A.7.12 Player-Facing Description (Website Canon)

"The Dak'Aridari were not born beneath Verasanth.

They were left there.

In the lightless strata where stone remembers
more than people do, they learned to see
without light, to move without sound, and to
listen to the city in ways others cannot.

They do not fear the dark.

They understand it.

The Ledger records them clearly.
The demon cannot navigate without them.

And somewhere beneath the city, there are
places that will only open to them."

### A.7.13 Design Rules for Dak'Aridari Content

- They always feel like they know something
  the player doesn't — even when the player
  is one
- Never explain their perception directly —
  show it through flavor text
- The Hollow-Deep must feel tragic, not just
  threatening — Dak'Aridari players should
  feel the "that could have been me" weight
- Never name the three tiers in player-facing
  text — let players discover the pattern
- The demon's resentment of them should be
  palpable in any encounter involving both
- The Scar is a threshold, not a destination —
  it points downward
- The Unspoken are never explained. Ever.

🌑 DAK’ARIDARI — FINAL VISUAL IDENTITY (Player-Facing Canon)
Depth Host — Those Who See What the City Forgot

🪨 1. Silhouette — Compact, Grounded, Built for Pressure
Dak’Aridari bodies are shaped by a world that pressed in from all sides.
- Slightly shorter on average than other Aridari
- Compact, efficient builds — nothing wasted
- Shoulders subtly forward, center of gravity low
- Head often angled as if listening to the stone
From a distance:
They look like they were built for spaces that push back.

🌑 2. Skin & Surface — Light-Absorbing, Depth-Shaped
Their skin tells the story of a people shaped by absence, pressure, and echo.
- Matte, light-absorbing surface
- Deep charcoal, muted slate, or dark earthen tones
- Subtle mineral undertones visible only at certain angles
- Occasional faint shimmer when vibrations pass through the environment
Texture:
Dense, compact — not rough, not soft.
Like something shaped under weight.
Distinctive:
Light doesn’t sit on them the way it does on others.

👁️ 3. Eyes — Reflective, Interpreting, Not Made for the Surface
This is the defining feature of the Dak’Aridari.
Their eyes don’t just see.
They interpret.
Iris qualities:
- Reflective
- Multi-layered
- Faintly luminescent in low light
- Colors: pale violet, dim blue, soft white glints
Pupils feel slightly “off” — too responsive, too adaptive, too aware.
Key behavior:
Their eyes don’t lock onto faces first.
They track:
- movement behind you
- shifts in airflow
- pressure changes
- echoes you can’t hear
NPC reaction:
“Why are they watching the wall…?”

🧱 4. Facial Structure — Pressure-Shaped, Not Monstrous
Their features carry the subtle imprint of the lower strata.
- Pronounced cheekbones
- Compact, functional jawlines
- Tightened facial planes
- Slightly narrowed eyes adapted for low light and echo-reading
Nothing grotesque.
Just… adapted.

🌑 5. Hair — Practical, Dark, Light-Absorbing
Hair reflects the environment they survived.
- Dark tones: black, deep gray, muted ash
- Slightly coarse or heavy texture
- Often tied back or kept controlled
- Some individuals’ hair seems to absorb light at the edges
Nothing decorative.
Everything functional.

⚖️ 6. Movement — Precise, Quiet, Spatially Intelligent
Dak’Aridari movement is the clearest sign they are not surface-born.
- Steps placed, not taken
- Instinctive avoidance of unnecessary sound
- Slight delay before movement — then exact, efficient action
- They move when the space allows it, not when people expect it
They don’t move like they’re being cautious.
They move like they’re listening.

🌑 7. Distinctive Trait (Across a Room)
You don’t notice them first.
You notice:
- the stillness
- the silence
- the sense that they’ve been there longer than you realized
And then:
They knew you were coming before you entered.

🧬 8. Optional Variation — Player Expression Within Identity
Players can lean into:
- Echo-Sensed: sharper posture, hyper-aware eyes
- Veil-Walked: lighter steps, harder to visually track
- Stone-Blooded: denser build, slower but immovable presence
Variations in:
- build density
- eye reflectivity
- subtle facial structure differences
But always:
They feel like they belong somewhere deeper than where they stand.

👁️ 9. How NPCs Read Them — The Quiet Unease
NPCs don’t think:
- “Scout”
- “Rogue”
They think:
“They know something about this place that I don’t.”
Reactions:
- Unease
- Respect
- Instinctive quiet
- A subtle urge to lower one’s voice

⚠️ 10. The Unsettling Edge — The Demon’s Need and Resentment
Your canon gives them a unique tension:
The demon needs them.
The demon resents them.
The city depends on them.
The surface misunderstands them.
Visually, this becomes:
- A presence that feels unreachable
- A gaze that’s never fully “here”
- A sense that part of them is always listening downward
Feeling:
“They’re not just here. They’re aware of more than here.”
