## NEW §A.10 — The Cambral: Full Canon

### A.10.1 Origin

The Cambral were here before anyone else.

Not because they were taken. Not because they
were trapped. Not because anything forced them.

They came because something beneath the earth
was calling them to build.

They are the Stone Host — the first builders,
the demon's unwilling workforce, the Ledger's
earliest witnesses, and the only race in
Verasanth whose purpose predates the city's
name.

They didn't know they were building a prison.
They didn't know they were building a city.
They only knew they were building.

### A.10.2 The Deep Resonance

The fifth force. The oldest. The one that
existed before the demon arrived, before the
Ledger awakened, before the Pale Fires burned,
before the Pathweaver adjusted its first street.

**The Deep Resonance is the universal principle
of structure seeking completion.**

Not a mind. Not a system. Not a will.

The pressure for form to stabilize.
The urge for things that are built to hold.
The background hum of the world insisting
that incomplete things become complete.

Before Verasanth, it existed everywhere —
unfocused, unnoticed, background force.

Then Verasanth happened.

A massive incomplete structure. Multiple forces
colliding — the demon's compulsion, the Ledger's
awakening, the Pale Fires, the Pathweaver's
first corrections. Instability at scale.

And the Deep Resonance did something it had
never done before:

**It anchored itself.**

Verasanth is now the loudest expression of
the Deep Resonance in existence. The city is
not just built — it is still trying to be
completed. Every structural shift, every
collapsing passage, every wrong wall is the
Deep Resonance still working.

The Cambral are the only ones who can feel it.

### A.10.3 What the Three Lines Believe

The Cambral do not fully understand the Deep
Resonance. Each line has its own interpretation:

**Resonant belief:**
*"The city is thinking. We can feel it forming."*

**Deep-Bound belief:**
*"Something down there is unfinished —
and dangerous."*

**Hollow-Forged belief:**
*"It never stopped building."*

None of them are fully right.

### A.10.4 What They Built Without Knowing

They were bound craftsmen. Not enslaved. Not
free. Compelled by resonance to build something
they believed was meaningful — a fortress, a
sanctuary, a city of purpose.

They did not know:
- The demon was imprisoned here
- The city was unfinished by design
- The Ledger was dormant beneath them
- The Pale Fires were waiting to wake

**Their tragedy: they built the prison without
knowing who the prisoner was.**

### A.10.5 The Three Cambral Lines

These are historical imprints — proximity to
different forces during the building era. They
are also player sub-types set at character
creation. Each line descends from Cambral who
worked near a different force.

Flag: cam_line

---

**The Resonant (Ledger-Touched)**
*"We feel when the city thinks."*

Built closest to the early mechanisms that
became the Ledger. Not changed intentionally —
imprinted. The Ledger's first attempts to
understand life left faint echoes in their
descendants' bones.

Traits: Sense structural instability. Detect
shifts in the city's processing. Feel the
Ledger's presence like a pressure change.
Stabilize buildings by instinct.

Role: Architects, maintainers, interpreters
of the city's structural state.

Flavor: *"The stone is steady.
The system beneath it is not."*

Flag value: "resonant"

---

**The Deep-Bound (Demon-Adjacent)**
*"We remember what worked below."*

Assigned to the deepest construction layers
under the demon's forced oversight. Proximity
to the demon's presence left them scarred, not
corrupted. Stronger, more stubborn, resistant
to magical influence, deeply suspicious of
anything "alive" in the city's systems.

Not evil. Scarred. There is a difference.

Traits: Extreme durability. Resistance to
magical influence and manipulation. Distrust
of the Ledger, the Pathweaver, and anything
that smells like system behavior.

Role: Enforcers, defenders, those who break
what shouldn't exist.

Flavor: *"This wasn't built right.
You can feel it."*

Flag value: "deep_bound"

---

**The Hollow-Forged (Pressure-Shaped)**
*"We endured what the city became."*

Trapped in the same collapse that created the
Dak'Aridari's descent — but where the Dak'
were reshaped by darkness and echo, the
Hollow-Forged were reshaped by pressure and
silence. Emerged centuries later, changed but
not monstrous. Slow, dense, immovable.

Traits: Immense physical density. Slow,
deliberate decision-making. Near-impossible
to dislodge once committed. They do not
move — so the world adjusts.

Role: Anchors, last-line stabilizers,
living pillars.

Flavor: *"You do not move.
So the world adjusts."*

Flag value: "hollow_forged"

### A.10.7 Relationship to Other Races

**With Dak'Aridari:**
Respect mixed with guilt. The Cambral know the
collapse that trapped the Dak'Aridari was partly
their fault. They built the layers that failed.

**With Pan'Aridari:**
Mutual fascination. The Pan'Aridari read the
city's flow. The Cambral read its bones. Together
they can interpret the city better than anyone.

**With Mal'Aridari:**
Deep admiration. The Mal'Aridari heal what the
Cambral cannot fix with stone. Philosophically
aligned — both see themselves as caretakers.

**With Ashborn:**
Caution. The Pale Fires hurt the Cambral's
internal resonance. They feel the Ashborn like
static in the stone.

### A.10.8 Why They Return Now

Because Verasanth is waking up. And when the
city stirs, the Cambral feel it before anyone.

They detect:
- Tremors in the foundations
- Shifts in the city's weight distribution
- The Ledger's growing awareness
- The demon's renewed desperation
- The Deep Resonance stirring again

They do not see themselves as citizens. They
see themselves as responsible.

They come not to rule or reclaim. They come
to stabilize what is about to fail.

### A.10.9 Cultural Identity

The Cambral do not have a unified culture in
the way other races do. The three lines
developed separately over centuries. What
they share:

**"The city is not finished."**

Every Cambral knows this. They may disagree
about what it means. They do not disagree
about the fact.

Behavioral traits across all lines:
- Speak less than they know
- Move deliberately, never wastefully
- Touch walls and floors when entering new spaces
- Feel physically uncomfortable in unstable structures
- Do not abandon things they have agreed to maintain

### A.10.10 Player-Facing Description (Website Canon)

"The Cambral were the first to answer the call
beneath Verasanth.

They came not for conquest, nor survival — but
because something in the deep stone resonated
with purpose.

They built what they did not understand.

And now, as the city begins to wake, they are
the only ones who can feel where it is
beginning to fail.

They did not shape the city.

They gave it form.

And something beneath it is still trying
to finish what they started."

### A.10.11 Design Rules for Cambral Content

- They feel structural problems before seeing them
- They carry guilt about the Dak'Aridari collapse —
  never let this be resolved easily
- The Deep Resonance is never explained to players
  directly — only felt through flavor text
- The Hollow-Forged emerged centuries late —
  they are ancient even among the Cambral
- The Deep-Bound distrust magic, systems, and
  anything that "thinks" — this creates friction
  with Ledger-adjacent content
- The city is still being built — every structural
  anomaly is the Deep Resonance at work
- Cambral players should feel responsible for
  the city's structural state, not just curious

🪨 CAMBRAL — FINAL VISUAL IDENTITY (Player-Facing Canon)
Stone Host — Those Who Feel the City Trying to Finish Itself

🧱 1. Silhouette — Broad, Grounded, Deliberate
Cambral bodies are shaped by purpose, not aesthetics.
- Tall or equal height to most races
- Broad shoulders, squared stance
- Weight distributed perfectly through the legs
- Movements slow, deliberate, never uncertain
- Every step feels committed
From a distance:
They look like they belong to the structure more than the people inside it.

🪨 2. Skin & Surface — Formed, Not Hardened
Their skin reflects the Deep Resonance’s influence — not stone, but shaped by the idea of structure.
Texture:
- Subtly dense
- Slightly matte
- Holds light rather than reflecting it
Color range:
- Granite gray
- Earthen brown
- Muted sandstone
- Deep slate
In some individuals:
- Faint geometric stress lines beneath the skin
- Subtle “grain” patterns like compressed material
Distinctive:
They don’t look hardened.
They look formed.

👁️ 3. Eyes — Reading Integrity, Not Emotion
Cambral eyes don’t read people first.
They read structures.
Colors:
- Deep amber
- Mineral gold
- Iron-gray
Light in their eyes feels steady — not reactive, not shifting.
Their gaze tracks:
- walls
- ceilings
- load points
- structural weaknesses
before it ever settles on a person.
NPC reaction:
“Why are they looking at the support beams…?”

🧱 4. Facial Structure — Solid, Composed, Intentional
Their features reflect the same principles as their bodies:
- Strong, defined lines
- Minimal but meaningful expressions
- A sense of composure, not coldness
They don’t emote quickly.
They evaluate first.

🪵 5. Hands — The Telltale Detail
This is the most Cambral thing about them.
Their hands are:
- Slightly broader
- Thicker at the joints
- Precise in movement
And they touch things:
- walls
- floors
- doorframes
Not absentmindedly.
To understand them.
It’s instinct — the Deep Resonance whispering through their bones.

⚖️ 6. Movement — Deliberate, Binding, Committed
Cambral movement is defined by intention.
- When they step, they mean it
- When they stop, they set
- They don’t reposition constantly like Pan’Aridari
- They don’t adapt mid-step like Dak’Aridari
Once they occupy space, that space feels decided.

🪨 7. Distinctive Trait (Across a Room)
You notice something before you notice them:
- A wall feels more stable
- A room feels quieter
- A structure feels less strained
Then you realize:
They’re standing there.
And the room feels like it’s holding together because of it.

🧬 8. Optional Variation — Player Expression Within Identity
Players can lean into:
Resonant
- Sharper gaze
- More reactive to environmental shifts
Deep-Bound
- Heavier build
- More rigid posture
Hollow-Forged
- Slower movement
- Immovable presence
Variations in:
- body density
- skin patterning
- visible “structural lines”
But always:
They feel like part of something larger than themselves.

👁️ 9. How NPCs Read Them — The Unspoken Trust
NPCs don’t think:
- “Warrior”
- “Craftsman”
They think:
“If something breaks… they’ll know why.”
Reactions:
- Quiet trust
- Unease (they notice things others ignore)
- Deference in unstable environments

⚠️ 10. The Weight They Carry — The Builder’s Guilt
Your canon gives them a unique emotional gravity:
They built the prison without knowing who the prisoner was.
This shows visually:
- A slight hesitation before entering unstable areas
- A habit of examining structures like they’re responsible for them
- A subtle heaviness — not physical, but moral
Feeling:
“If this fails… it matters to them personally.”
