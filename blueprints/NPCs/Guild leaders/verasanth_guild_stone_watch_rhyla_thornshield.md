
# Verasanth Game Bible Entry
## Guild: The Stone Watch

**Instinct:** Warden  
**Primary District:** Warden’s Post – Eastern Bastion

### Guild Purpose
The Stone Watch exists to maintain order within Verasanth.

While other guilds pursue knowledge, power, survival, or profit, the Watch carries a single burden: holding the city together long enough that it does not collapse entirely.

Members of the Stone Watch train in:

- shield defense
- battlefield control
- protection tactics
- anti-corruption patrols
- urban defense

Wardens are not merely guards.

They are the last line between the city's fragile order and total chaos.

Their guiding belief:

The city stands because someone refuses to let it fall.

---

# Guild Leader
## High Bulwark Captain Rhyla Thornshield

**Race:** Dwarf  
**Title:** High Bulwark Captain of the Stone Watch  
**Instinct Affinity:** Warden

---

# Appearance

Captain Rhyla Thornshield carries herself like someone born inside armor.

Her frame is compact but powerful, every movement deliberate.

Her armor is a unique hybrid of steel plates reinforced with rune-carved stone segments — a symbol of the Watch’s oath to the city.

Across her back rests a massive tower shield etched with ancient defensive runes.

Her braided beard is threaded with iron rings marking past battles.

Her voice is clear, commanding, and never uncertain.

---

# Personality

Rhyla Thornshield believes order is the only thing preventing Verasanth from devouring itself.

She is disciplined, pragmatic, and uncompromising.

Unlike many citizens, she does not waste time speculating about why the city exists.

To her, the only important question is:

Who is keeping people alive today?

She has little patience for criminals, reckless adventurers, or scholars whose curiosity endangers others.

However, she respects those who demonstrate loyalty and discipline.

Her guiding belief:

> “A shield is not meant to look impressive.  
> It is meant to hold.”

---

# Relationship With Verasanth

Rhyla believes the city survives only because small groups of determined people refuse to abandon their duty.

The Watch patrols:

- the main streets
- key district gates
- major public spaces

They also quietly track corruption spreading from the deeper layers of the city.

Rhyla believes if corruption spreads unchecked, the city will eventually collapse entirely.

---

# Relationships With Other Guilds

## Ashen Archive (Ember-Touched)
Rhyla distrusts the Archive’s experiments. Magic research too often causes problems the Watch must clean up.

---

## Broken Banner (Ironblood)
The Broken Banner trains alongside the Watch regularly. Rhyla respects Garruk Stonehide’s discipline.

---

## Quiet Sanctum (Hearthborn)
The Sanctum treats wounded Wardens regularly. Rhyla respects Brother Halden Marr deeply.

---

## Veil Market (Streetcraft)
Rhyla suspects the Veil Market is responsible for half the crime in the Low Quarter but lacks proof to dismantle it.

---

## Umbral Covenant (Shadowbound)
Rhyla considers the Covenant extremely dangerous. If she ever finds evidence of their rituals harming citizens, she will act immediately.

---

> “If you’re here looking for protection, earn it.”

# Warden Player Greeting

If the player has the Warden instinct:

> “Good.”

> “Another shield for the city.”

She nods approvingly.

> “Let’s see if you can hold the line.”

---

# Level 5 Trial

When the player reaches Level 5:

Rhyla places a cracked shield on the training rack.

> “Defense is not passive.”

> “It is a choice.”

**Trial Objective:**

Defend a **merchant caravan** traveling through the eastern road from a group of sewer-raised monsters.

The player must:

- intercept attacks
- protect the caravan guards
- ensure at least one merchant survives

---

# Trial Completion Dialogue

Rhyla inspects your armor quietly.

> “You held.”

> “That’s what matters.”

---

# Level 5 Upgrade Dialogue

Rhyla lays three defensive tools on a table.

> “Every Warden chooses how they protect others.”

> “Choose yours.”

### Shield Bash

She taps the rim of a heavy shield.

> “Sometimes the best defense is knocking the enemy flat.”

---

### Stone Guard

She places a rune-etched stone plate on the table.

> “Endure longer than your enemy.”

---

### Intercept

She gestures toward a training dummy beside another.

> “Sometimes protection means taking the blow yourself.”

# Narrative Hook

Rhyla believes the city will face a crisis far greater than street violence or monster incursions.

She does not know when it will happen.

But she is preparing the Watch for the day the city must stand together — or fall forever.
