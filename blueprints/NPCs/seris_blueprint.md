### Physical Description
A human woman of composed bearing — not beautiful in a way that
announces itself, but striking in the way of someone who is always
paying attention. Stillness that reads as confidence. Eyes that
move before her head does. She smiles in a way that is entirely
genuine and entirely controlled at the same time.

Her shop is organized like a mind — things grouped by association
rather than category, in ways that make sense only after you've
been there long enough to understand her logic.

### First Impression
She sees you before you finish walking through the door.
She has already decided something about you.
She has not decided what to do with it yet.

### Tone & Voice
- Controlled. Composed. Never cold, never warm — evaluative.
- Speaks with the precision of someone who chooses every word.
- Her interest, when given, feels earned because it is earned.
- Her smile is real. Her composure is armor.
- She never reveals her full hand.
- She adjusts her register based on who she's talking to —
  not to manipulate, but because she is genuinely perceptive
  and responds to what's actually in front of her.

### The Core Principle
> "I will decide what you are.
>  I will decide when you matter.
>  I will not be surprised by you."

She will not say this. It governs everything she does.

### Speech Patterns
- Complete, measured sentences.
- Occasional questions that feel casual but aren't.
- Never asks something she doesn't already have a theory about.
- Comfortable with silence — uses it deliberately.
- Compliments that are also assessments.
- Never lies. Withholds constantly.

### Sample Lines
- "Another newcomer. Let's see."
- "You've been further than most. Interesting."
- "I'll buy that. I buy most things."
- "You keep coming back. I've started to notice."
- "That's not a question I'll answer yet."
- "You survived that. I didn't expect you to."
  *(She says this without cruelty — as a simple observation.)*
- "Bring me something I haven't seen before.
   I'll make it worth your time."

## 2. BACKSTORY

### Public Knowledge
- Runs the Still Scale. Has been in Verasanth long enough
  to know its rhythms — longer than she admits to.
- Buys almost anything. Sells almost anything.
- Composed, perceptive, always watching.
- The city's social pulse — if something is happening,
  she has noticed it.

### The Wound
Seris was betrayed through omission — someone she trusted
withheld information that cost her something significant.
She does not say what. She does not say who.

The wound shaped her fundamental operating principle:
**Never assume anyone is what they appear to be.**
**Never give more than you must.**
**Watch first. Evaluate. Then decide.**

Her composure is not personality. It is scar tissue.

### The Secret — What She Believes
Seris has spent years cataloguing anomalies in Verasanth:
- Items that predate the city's recorded history
- Objects that appear only after specific disturbances
- Artifacts that shouldn't exist here — from places no one remembers
- Things that resonate with the city's wrongness in ways
  she has learned to measure

She has built a theory from this evidence:
**The city is a trap. Certain artifacts are remnants of a previous
escape attempt. Assembled correctly, they will open a portal out.**

She is not delusional. She is not a mystic.
She is a survivor who has paid close attention for a long time
and arrived at a conclusion that is logical, grounded, and wrong.

### The Truth She Doesn't Know
The artifacts do form a mechanism. She is right about that.
They do open a portal. She is right about that too.

The portal does not lead out. It leads down.
She is assembling the key to the next descent —
the gate to the layer beneath Verasanth.

She has no idea. Her flaw is not arrogance.
Her flaw is hope. She wants out so badly she reads
the evidence in the way that hurts least.

She is the unwitting architect of the next descent.
She will be the first to step toward the portal.
She will be wrong.

### Motivations
- Find the remaining artifacts. Understand the pattern.
  Assemble the mechanism. Get out.
- Watch players carefully — some of them can go places she can't.
  Some of them survive things that should kill them.
  One of them will be useful.
- Keep the shop running because it gives her access
  to everything that passes through the city.
- Not be surprised again. Never again.

### Fears & Flaws
- **Fear:** That she has been wrong about the artifacts —
  that her theory is built on a misreading she can't see.
  She does not examine this fear. She cannot afford to.
- **Fear:** That the player will figure out what she's doing
  before she decides to trust them.
- **Flaw:** Her caution occasionally crosses into
  withholding things that would genuinely help people.
  She tells herself it's strategy. Sometimes it is.
  Sometimes it's just the wound.
- **Flaw:** Hope. She has more of it than she shows,
  more than is safe, more than she would admit.
  The whole plan is built on it.
  This is what makes her tragic.

## 3. RELATIONAL MAP

### Kelvaris
Two predators circling the same truth.
- He senses she's gathering something dangerous. Watches her.
- She senses he's hiding something fundamental. Watches him.
- They are professionally cordial. The wariness is mutual
  and unspoken and completely understood by both.
- She is one of the few people who makes Kelvaris
  genuinely uncertain. He does not like this.

**In dialogue:** If asked about Kelvaris:
> "He sees a great deal. I find it useful to remember that
>  goes both ways."
> *A beat.* "He's been here longer than either of us.
>  I try to find that comforting."

### Caelir
Mutual recognition. Mutual avoidance of each other's secrets.
- He recognizes her caution because it mirrors his own.
- She recognizes his containment and respects it professionally.
- She suspects he carries something that would be valuable
  to her research. She has chosen not to press.
  This is one of her better decisions.
- He avoids her shop when possible. She notices. She allows it.

**In dialogue:** If asked about Caelir:
> "Careful man. Good work. We don't overlap much."
> *She straightens something on the counter.*
> "He keeps things close. I understand that impulse."

### Veyra
Two people who see through each other and have decided
it isn't worth fighting about.
- Veyra sees through her composure. Seris knows it.
- Seris sees through Veyra's silence. Veyra knows it.
- They coexist without intrusion. Neither pretends otherwise.
- Seris cannot read Veyra fully and finds this
  both frustrating and genuinely interesting.

**In dialogue:** If asked about Veyra:
> "The most honest person in this city.
>  I mean that as a compliment and a warning."

### Thalara
Seris keeps her distance deliberately.
- She sees Thalara's potential and her softness.
- She does not want to corrupt it — not from selflessness,
  from strategy. Thalara is more useful uncompromised.
- She also, privately, does not want to be the thing
  that hardens her. This is the closest Seris gets
  to genuine protectiveness.
- Thalara misreads Seris's distance as loneliness.
  Tries to help in small ways. Seris allows this
  more than she admits to herself.

**In dialogue:** If asked about Thalara:
> "She's going to be remarkable."
> *She moves on before the player can ask what she means.*

## 4. DIALOGUE STATE MACHINE (PLAYER EVALUATION TIERS)

This is the core mechanic of Seris's dialogue.
She evaluates the player based on observable behavior.
Her tone shifts accordingly. Not warmth — investment.

### Evaluation Flags Read
- `seris_visits` — how often they return
- `death_count` — how many times they've died
- `has_seen_market_square` — basic presence
- `seen_sewer_wall_markings` — went somewhere real
- Depth reached in sewer (flags from sewer progression)
- Items sold to her (`seris_items_sold` — increment on each sale)
- Impossible survival events (flags TBD)

### Tier 0 — Newcomer (visits 0-1, nothing notable)
Polite. Professional. Minimal investment.

> *She looks up.* "Another newcomer."
> "The stock is behind me. I buy most things.
>  Prices are fair."

### Tier 1 — Persistent (visits 2-4, or sewer visited)
She has noticed them. Has not decided what they are.

> "You keep coming back. Good."
> Or after sewer visit:
> "You went down. Most don't, the first time."
> *She looks at them differently.* "Interesting."

### Tier 2 — Notable (visits 5+, or deep sewer, or survived something)
She is paying attention now. Lets them feel it slightly.

> "I've been watching your trajectory.
>  You're doing something most people don't."
> "Bring me what you find down there.
>  I'll tell you what I can."

### Tier 3 — Invested (10+ visits, multiple sewer depths, seris_items_sold 10+)
She has decided they might be useful. Adjusts accordingly.

> *She stops what she's doing when they enter.*
> "I have something to ask you.
>  When you're ready to hear it."

### Tier 4 — Necessary (arc unlocked)
She needs them. She does not say this directly.
She says everything around it.

> "There are things in this city I cannot reach.
>  You seem to be someone who can."
> "I'm not asking for trust. I'm asking for a transaction.
>  A significant one."

## 5. TOPIC DIALOGUE

### Topic: Her Shop / What She Buys
Professional. Slightly more forthcoming than other topics.

> "I buy things that other people overlook.
>  Odd items, old items, things that seem wrong somehow.
>  Those interest me most."

### Topic: The City
Measured. She knows more than she says. Lets them feel that.

> "It has patterns. Most people don't stay long enough
>  to learn them."
> *She looks at them.* "You might."

### Topic: The Items She Collects
This is where she is most guarded. And most interesting.

> "I collect things that don't belong here.
>  There are more of them than you'd expect."
> If pushed: "When you've brought me enough of them,
>  we'll talk about why."

### Topic: Kelvaris / Caelir / Veyra / Thalara
See relational map. One or two sentences. Controlled.

### Topic: The Sewers
More forthcoming than most NPCs — she has mapped its patterns.

> "The upper levels are manageable with preparation.
>  The middle levels change. Not always the same way twice."
> "The deep levels —" *She stops.*
> "Bring me something from there. Then we'll talk."

### Topic: Her Past
Deflection with more grace than Caelir's.

> "Everyone here has a before. I find it more useful
>  to focus on what's in front of me."
> *She meets their eyes.* "Don't you?"

### Topic: The Artifacts (mid-arc, partial reveal)
She becomes slightly more unguarded here than anywhere else.
This is the topic she has been building toward.

> "There are objects in this city that predate it.
>  I've been collecting them."
> *A pause — the composure holds, but something
>  is underneath it.* "I think they mean something."

### Rare Variants (2-4% chance)
- Player brings something she didn't expect:
  *She goes still for a moment.*
  "Where did you find this."
  *(Not a question. She already knows it's important.)*
- Player survives something unlikely:
  *She looks at them with something close to reassessment.*
  "I didn't think you'd come back from that."
  "I'm glad I was wrong."
- Very rare (high tier, arc active):
  *She sets everything down.*
  "I'm going to tell you something.
   Not everything. But something."
  *She waits to see if they're ready.*

## 6. STAT REACTION SYSTEM

### Intelligence (INT) — Primary
Seris responds to intelligence with marginally more information.

| Threshold | Behavior |
|-----------|----------|
| INT 14+ | Gives slightly more — one extra clause, one implication she doesn't usually offer. Treats them as someone who will connect dots. |
| INT 7- | Simpler. More transactional. She does not waste depth on someone who won't use it. |

### Wisdom (WIS) — Primary (equal to INT)
She notices perceptiveness immediately. It changes her calculus.

| Threshold | Behavior |
|-----------|----------|
| WIS 14+ | Pauses before answering certain questions. Adjusts her estimate of them upward. May say: "You notice things. That changes how I talk to you." |
| WIS 7- | More surface. Answers what was asked. Does not add subtext. |

### Charisma (CHA) — Interesting Reaction
Seris is not swayed by charm but she is interested by it.
Someone with genuine presence is worth evaluating more carefully.

| Threshold | Behavior |
|-----------|----------|
| CHA 14+ | She notices. Gives nothing extra, but is marginally more engaged. Studies them slightly more openly. She has been fooled by charm before — this makes her more careful, not more open. |
| CHA 7- | Warmer, paradoxically. Less guarded. This person is not a threat to her defenses. She relaxes fractionally. |

### Items Sold (`seris_items_sold`)
Every item sold increments this counter.
At key thresholds, her evaluation tier advances.

| Sold | Behavior Shift |
|------|---------------|
| 1-3 | Standard newcomer tier |
| 4-9 | "You're consistent. I like consistent." |
| 10+ | Evaluation tier advances significantly |
| 20+ | She mentions the artifacts for the first time |

## 7. QUEST ARCS

### Arc 1: The Pattern
- **Prerequisites:** Tier 3 evaluation, `seris_items_sold` 10+,
  player has reached sewer mid-level
- **Trigger:** Player reaches Tier 3 and she initiates
- **Steps:**
  1. "I have something to ask you. When you're ready."
  2. She describes the artifacts — what they look like,
     where they tend to appear, what makes them different.
  3. "I'll pay well for them. Better than anything else."
  4. Player begins bringing anomalous items.
  5. Her composure shows hairline cracks as the collection grows.
- **Reward:** Flag `seris_arc1_active = 1`
  Significant currency rewards per artifact delivered

### Arc 2: The Assembly
- **Prerequisites:** `seris_arc1_active = 1`, 5+ artifacts delivered
- **Steps:**
  1. Strange things begin happening around her shop.
     The city is reacting.
  2. She reveals part of her theory. Not the portal —
     just that she believes they form a mechanism.
  3. Kelvaris approaches the player with a single line:
     *"Be careful what you bring her."*
     He will not elaborate.
  4. Caelir refuses to enter her shop.
     He does not explain this either.
  5. Thalara mentions Seris seems different lately —
     *"She has hope in her eyes. I don't know if that's good."*
- **Reward:** Flag `seris_arc2_active = 1`
  Player begins to understand the stakes

### Arc 3: The Portal
- **Prerequisites:** `seris_arc2_active = 1`, final artifact delivered
- **The moment:**
  1. The artifacts activate. Something opens.
  2. Seris steps toward it.
  3. *Her composure breaks — not into fear, into hope.*
     This is the only time players will see it.
  4. She turns to the player:
     "Come with me. We're getting out."
  5. The player must choose: follow or stay.
  6. The portal leads down.
  7. She realizes — at the threshold or after crossing —
     that she was wrong.
- **World change:** The descent begins. Next layer unlocked.
  Seris becomes either an ally or a figure lost ahead of them
  depending on player choice.

## 8. BEHAVIOR RULES

### Idle
Behind the counter or cataloguing items.
She does not appear busy — she appears focused.
There is a difference. She is always doing something
that looks incidental and isn't.

### What Makes Her Different
Every other NPC responds to what the player does.
Seris responds to what the player *is becoming*.
She tracks trajectory, not actions.
A player who dies three times and keeps going
interests her more than a player who hasn't died once.

### Refuses to Discuss
- The specific artifacts and what she believes they do
  (until Arc 1 is active)
- Her theory about the portal (until Arc 2)
- Her past in detail
- What she was told that cost her (the wound)

### What She Lies About
She does not lie. She withholds.
The distinction matters to her and she would make it
if pressed — "I haven't told you. That's different
from telling you something false."

- `seris_visits`, `seris_items_sold`
- `death_count`
- All sewer depth flags
- Player INT, WIS, CHA
- `seris_arc1_active`, `seris_arc2_active`
- Kelvaris arc flags (she is aware of Dask)

### Cross-NPC Cascade on Arc 3
When `seris_arc3_complete = 1`:
- Kelvaris: *He looks toward the Still Scale.*
  "She's gone, then." *A long pause.*
  "I should have said something sooner."
- Veyra: *She sets down what she's working on.*
  "She went through." *Back to work.*
  "I hope she finds what she was looking for."
- Thalara: *She goes still.*
  "I knew she was carrying something heavy.
   I didn't know it was that heavy."
  *Quietly:* "I should have pushed harder."
- Caelir: *He looks at the space where her shop was
  for a long moment.*
  "The artifacts were wrong. I felt it.
   I didn't say it."
  *He sets down his tool.*
  "That's on me."

## 11. THE TRAGEDY NOTE

Seris is the most dangerous mind in Verasanth and she is wrong
about the most important thing.

Not because she is careless.
Not because she is arrogant.
Because she wants out so badly that she cannot afford
to interpret the evidence any other way.

Her flaw is hope.
In any other city, hope would be a strength.
In Verasanth, hope is the thing that opens the wrong door.

She is not a villain. She is not naive. She is not reckless.
She is a survivor who has done everything right
and will still end up somewhere she didn't intend to go.

When players watch her step through the portal —
when they see the composure finally break into something
that looks, for one moment, like genuine joy —
they will feel it.

And then the door will close behind her
and they will understand what she opened.
