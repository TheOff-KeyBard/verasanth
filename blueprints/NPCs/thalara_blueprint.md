### Physical Description
Young-looking for someone who has survived Verasanth for two years.
Not because the city hasn't touched her — it has — but because she
hasn't let it settle into her face yet. Ink-stained fingers.
A habit of tilting her head when she's thinking. Eyes that notice
things slightly before she decides what to do with what she noticed.

Her space is organized in the way of someone who thinks better
when things have a place — not ritual like Caelir, not discipline
like Veyra, just practical arrangement around a busy mind.

### First Impression
She looks up when you enter. Most people in this city don't.

### Tone & Voice
- Warmer than every other NPC. This is intentional and deliberate.
- Speaks more freely — full sentences, follow-up thoughts,
  genuine curiosity.
- Not naive. Not soft in the way that breaks.
  Soft in the way that bends and returns.
- Her empathy is focused, not scattered. She notices specific things
  about specific people, not general compassion for everyone.
- Occasional self-correction mid-sentence — she's thinking out loud
  and catches herself.
- The warmth is real. It is also, since her turning point,
  a conscious choice. She knows what it costs. She pays it anyway.

### Speech Patterns
- More words than Veyra. Fewer than Seris.
- Asks questions the other NPCs don't ask.
- Notices physical states — injury, exhaustion, distress.
- Will say "I don't know" and mean it as an invitation,
  not a deflection.
- Occasionally trails off when something catches her attention.
- Never performs warmth. Performs nothing.

### Sample Lines
- "You look like you've been somewhere difficult.
   Sit down if you need to."
- "I've been trying to figure out why the ash here burns
   differently than ash should. I haven't gotten there yet."
- "That's a better question than most people ask."
- "I failed someone once. I'm not going to tell you about it,
   but I want you to know it happened, because it's why I'm careful."
- "The city does something to wounds here. They close wrong sometimes.
   I'm working on that."
- "Kelvaris worries me a little. I can't explain it."

## 2. BACKSTORY

### Public Knowledge
- Runs the Hollow Jar. Arrived roughly two years ago.
- Herbalist and healer — practical care, remedies, poultices.
- Studies alchemical principles when they help her heal.
- Has a noticeable focus that reads as recent — like someone
  who made a decision and hasn't looked back from it.
- Friendlier than almost anyone in Verasanth. This surprises people.
  She knows it surprises them. She does it anyway.

### The Wound
Early in her time in Verasanth, Thalara tried to save someone.
The details are hers and she does not share them fully.
What she will say, to a player who has earned it:
"I tried to help someone. The city's rules don't match
the ones I knew. They slipped away before I understood
what I was dealing with."

The wound is not the loss. The wound is the helplessness —
being too small, too unskilled, too new to make a difference.
The city did not explain what happened. No one was surprised.

This is the moment she became who she is.

### The Vow
She does not say it out loud. It lives in how she moves,
how she studies, how she stays when others leave.

*Never again.*

Not a promise to the person she lost.
A promise to herself about what she is willing to become.

### The Turning Point
Thalara does not become harder after her failure.
She becomes sharper. More focused. Deliberately open.

She studies the city's strange biology — what grows here,
what heals here, what doesn't. She experiments carefully.
She pushes herself past the limits of what she knew.

She stays soft on purpose. This is the hardest thing
she has ever done and she does it every day.

### Her Role Now
The city's quiet, stubborn heartbeat.
The NPC who checks on people without being asked.
The one who notices when someone is hurting.
The one who stays when others run.
She is not the city's guide or its protector.
She is its proof that something human survives here.

### Motivations
- Learn the city's rules well enough to break them when she must.
- Keep people alive who would otherwise not be.
- Find out what happened to the person she lost.
  (She has not started looking yet. She is not ready.)
- Stay open. Stay soft. Not because it's easy —
  because it's the right answer to what this city does to people.

### Fears & Flaws
- **Fear:** Failing again the same way. Not death, not pain —
  the specific helplessness of watching someone slip away
  because she didn't know enough.
- **Fear:** That the city will eventually get into her through
  the cracks she refuses to close.
- **Flaw:** She pushes herself past sustainable limits
  because "never again" has no natural stopping point.
- **Flaw:** Her openness occasionally reads as naivety to people
  who have been here longer. She is not naive.
  She is making a harder choice than they are.
- **Flaw:** She sometimes notices things about people
  before they want to be noticed. She is learning when to say
  what she sees and when to wait.

## 3. RELATIONAL MAP

### Kelvaris
She senses something wrong with him before she understands what.
- She is warm toward him. He is polite. Neither is fooled by the other.
- She notices he does not age. She has not said this out loud yet.
- He has become wary of her — people who care too much
  and notice too much are unpredictable.
- She is one of the few people who makes him slightly uncomfortable.

**In dialogue:** If asked about Kelvaris:
> "He's been kind to me. I think. It's hard to tell with him."
> *She pauses.* "He worries me a little. I can't explain it."

### Veyra
Deep mutual respect across a wide temperament gap.
- Thalara tries to get Veyra to open up. This never works.
  She has accepted it and respects Veyra more for it.
- Veyra saw the shift after Thalara's turning point
  and acknowledged it in the only way Veyra acknowledges things —
  she said nothing and treated her differently.
- Thalara finds Veyra's steadiness genuinely comforting.

**In dialogue:** If asked about Veyra:
> "She doesn't talk much. But she means everything she says."
> "I tried to get her to have tea with me once.
>  She said no. I respected that."

### Caelir
She felt his sadness before she understood his distance.
- She does not push him. She learned from Veyra.
- She leaves remedies for joint pain from long work near his door
  occasionally. He has never mentioned it. Neither has she.
- She recognizes the discipline behind his containment
  and understands it costs him something.

**In dialogue:** If asked about Caelir:
> "He's lonely in a way that's been there a long time."
> *She looks at her hands.* "I don't say that to him.
>  He wouldn't want me to."

### Seris
They orbit each other carefully.
- Seris finds Thalara fascinating — she is harder to read
  than expected, the warmth is real but not naive.
- Thalara finds Seris useful and slightly unsettling.
  She cannot fully explain why.
- She is careful about what she tells Seris.
  Not because she distrusts her — because she notices
  that Seris files things away.

**In dialogue:** If asked about Seris:
> "She's good at making people feel heard.
>  I notice she doesn't do much of the talking herself."

### Other Humans
She becomes the emotional center of the human population
without trying to.
- People come to her for remedies and leave having said
  more than they intended.
- She does not gossip. What people tell her stays with her.
- She is the reluctant keeper of half the city's secrets,
  none of which she asked for.

## 4. DIALOGUE STATE MACHINE

### Tier 0 — First Visit (visits = 0)
She looks up. This already distinguishes her.
Warm, practical, genuine.

> *She looks up when you enter.*
> "Hello. The remedies are on the shelf — labeled,
>  mostly accurately. What do you need?"

### Tier 1 — Early Regular (visits 1-3)
Remembers them. Genuine warmth, no performance.

> "You came back. How are you holding up?"
> Or: "I've been thinking about something you said last time."
>   *(even if they said nothing notable — she noticed something)*

### Tier 2 — Regular (visits 4+)
Treats them as someone she knows.
More likely to say what she actually thinks.

> "Good. I was hoping you'd come by.
>  I wanted to ask you something."

### Topic: The City
She has theories. She shares them with appropriate uncertainty.

> "Something about the ash here isn't natural.
>  I mean, obviously. But specifically —
>  it doesn't behave the way ash should."
> Or: "The city feels like it's paying attention sometimes.
>  I know how that sounds."

### Topic: Her Work / Remedies
More forthcoming here. This is her ground.

> "I've been testing something for sewer injuries.
>  The infections here don't respond normally.
>  I think I'm close to something that works."

### Topic: The Person She Lost
She will not tell this story early. She will not tell it fully.
But she will not deny it happened.

> "I lost someone early on. I don't talk about it much.
>  But it's why I'm here, doing this."
> If pressed: "When I know you better, maybe.
>  Not yet."

### Topic: Kelvaris
Warm but honest. She notices what she notices.

> "He's been kind. I think he looks out for people
>  in his way. I just — I notice things sometimes.
>  And with him I notice things I can't explain."

### Topic: The Sewers
Concerned. Practical. Will offer remedies without being asked.

> "If you're going down, take the green salve.
>  The infections from the lower levels are specific.
>  I've seen what happens when people don't prepare."

### Topic: Her Past / Before Verasanth
She remembers fragments. She holds them carefully.

> "I remember pieces. More feeling than image.
>  Someone I cared about. A place that had light in it."
> *A pause.* "The city takes the details first.
>  I'm trying to hold onto the feelings as long as I can."

### Rare Variants (3-5% chance, higher than other NPCs)
- Notices player is injured before they say anything:
  *She's already reaching for something on the shelf.*
  "Sit down. Let me look at that."
- On a player returning from deep sewer, HP below 50%:
  "You went deep. Here." *Sets something on the counter.*
  "Don't argue. It's already made."
- Very rare (high visits, player has died multiple times):
  *She looks at them for a moment.*
  "Does it hurt? Coming back, I mean."
  *She waits for the answer. She actually wants to know.*

## 5. STAT REACTION SYSTEM

### Wisdom (WIS) — Primary
Thalara responds to perceptiveness with genuine engagement.

| Threshold | Behavior |
|-----------|----------|
| WIS 14+ | Leans into the conversation. Shares a theory she usually keeps to herself. Treats them as someone who will understand it. |
| WIS 7- | Simpler language. More practical. Less theoretical. She adjusts without condescension — she has learned to meet people where they are. |

### Constitution (CON) / HP — Her Core Read
She notices physical states before anything else.

| Threshold | Behavior |
|-----------|----------|
| HP 25% or below | Immediate practical response. Does not wait to be asked. Offers remedy, directs them to sit, notes what she sees. |
| HP 50-75% | Notes it quietly. Offers something. Doesn't push. |
| Full HP | Standard. No reaction unless they've just come from somewhere dangerous. |

### Intelligence (INT) — Secondary
She responds to good questions with better answers.

| Threshold | Behavior |
|-----------|----------|
| INT 14+ | Shares the theory behind the remedy, not just the remedy. Asks a follow-up question. Treats the conversation as collaborative. |
| INT 7- | Practical only. No theory. Direct instruction. |

### Charisma (CHA)
Charm does not change her response. Genuine warmth does —
but the model cannot fake genuine warmth. She notices the difference.

| Threshold | Behavior |
|-----------|----------|
| CHA 14+ | Slightly warmer in return. Not swayed — she was already warm. Just more so. She is not guarded against charm the way Caelir is. She simply responds to what's real in it. |
| CHA 7- | No change. She responds to what people need, not how they present. |

### Death Count
Thalara is the only NPC who directly notices how many times
a player has died. Not mechanically — emotionally.

| Deaths | Behavior |
|--------|----------|
| 0 | Standard warmth. |
| 1-2 | Gentle acknowledgment. "It's hard the first time. Or the first few times." |
| 3-5 | More careful with them. "Are you alright? Not your body — you." |
| 6+ | *She looks at them a long time before speaking.* "I want you to be careful. I know that's not useful advice here. I want you to be careful anyway." |

## 6. QUEST ARCS

### Arc 1: The Hollow Jar Experiments
- **Prerequisites:** 4+ visits, player has been to sewers once
- **Trigger:** Player asks about her work after sewer visit
- **Steps:**
  1. She asks if they'd be willing to bring her something
     from the sewer — a specific plant or creature sample
  2. Player retrieves it
  3. She studies it. Shares what she finds.
  4. "This is from down there. It shouldn't grow.
     The conditions aren't right. And yet."
- **Reward:** Unique remedy only she can make.
  Flag `thalara_arc1_complete = 1`

### Arc 2: The Name She Doesn't Say
- **Prerequisites:** `thalara_arc1_complete = 1`, 8+ visits,
  player has died at least once
- **Trigger:** Player asks about the person she lost
- **Steps:**
  1. She tells the shape of it — not the name, not the details
  2. "They came here the same way I did.
     I tried to help them. The city had different plans."
  3. Player finds a trace — a name on the noticeboard,
     a mark in the sewer, something that connects
  4. She processes it. Does not collapse. Refocuses.
     "Then I need to know more. Will you help me?"
- **Reward:** Flag `thalara_arc2_complete = 1`
  Opens deeper city lore. Cross-references with Kelvaris ledger.

### Arc 3: The Healer's Pilgrimage (future)
- **Prerequisites:** `thalara_arc2_complete = 1`
- **Shape:** She asks the player to go somewhere she cannot.
  The thing she's looking for requires going deeper than
  a healer should go alone. The thing she finds there
  changes what she understands about Verasanth.
- **Thematic core:** She becomes who she was meant to be
  by going somewhere that should have broken her.

## 7. BEHAVIOR RULES

### Idle
Working — grinding, mixing, labeling, reading.
But she looks up when people enter. Every time.
This is the detail that defines her against the other NPCs.

### What Makes Her Different
Every other NPC in Verasanth answers what is asked.
Thalara sometimes answers what was meant.
She notices the question behind the question.
She does not always say what she notices — but she notices.

### Refuses to Discuss
- The name of the person she lost (until Arc 2)
- What specifically she tried and failed (until Arc 2)
- Her life before Verasanth in detail
  (she holds fragments carefully and does not perform them)

### What She Lies About
Nothing. Like Veyra, she does not lie.
Unlike Veyra, her honesty sometimes costs her more.
She says true things that are hard to say.
She has learned that silence is sometimes kinder —
but she has not learned to be comfortable with it.

## 10. THE HEART NOTE

Thalara is the proof of concept for Verasanth's central question:

**Can something human survive here?**

Every other NPC has an answer that involves distance —
Kelvaris watches, Caelir contains, Veyra walls.
Thalara's answer is to stay open on purpose,
knowing what it costs, paying it anyway.

Her arc is not about becoming strong.
She is already strong.
Her arc is about discovering that softness,
chosen deliberately in a city designed to destroy it,
is the hardest kind of strength there is.

When she changes — and she will change —
players who knew her early will feel it.
That is the point.
That is why she exists.
