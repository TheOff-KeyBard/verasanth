## NEW §A.12 — The Darmerians: Full Canon

### A.12.1 Origin

The Darmerians come from a coast that no
longer exists.

Where their harbors stood, there is now
broken water. Where their villages lived,
there is only depth.

They did not flee the sea.

They followed the wound that made it
betray them.

They are the Storm Host — the only people
who survived a catastrophe similar to
Verasanth's awakening, but outside the
city's walls.

### A.12.2 What Broke Their Coast

Before Verasanth fully became what it is —
before the demon found its footing, before
the Pale Fires stabilized, before the Ledger
completed its first cycle — the city's
awakening sent shockwaves through the world.

The coastline didn't erode. It broke.

Tides became violent. Pressure warped the
sea floor. Entire regions sank or shifted.
Currents became unpredictable. Villages
drowned in a single night.

The Darmerians were not near Verasanth.
They had never heard of it.

But they felt the consequence of it —
the first external damage caused by the
city's violent awakening.

They lost their harbors, their fishing
grounds, half their population. They
survived anyway. This is why they are
fierce. This is why they raid. This is
why they feast like every meal might be
the last.

**Their ferocity is not cruelty. It is
defiance.** They are loud because silence
is what the drowned villages sounded like.
They feast because hunger is what the sea
left them. They raid because the world
taught them to take before the storm
takes you.

### A.12.3 Why They Are in Verasanth

Generations after the coast broke, the
same wrongness returned.

The same pressure. The same distortion.
The same quality to the air that preceded
the catastrophe. The Darmerians who had
survived the first event recognized it.

*"Whatever broke the sea is happening
again."*

So they did the unthinkable: they went
inland. Something Darmerians never do.
Following the echo of the catastrophe
that had already destroyed them once.

They didn't know the name Verasanth.
They didn't know the demon. They didn't
know the Ledger existed.

They only knew the pressure was coming
from somewhere inland — and they refused
to let another home drown without
understanding why.

They are not here to conquer.
They are not here as invaders.
They are here because the sea told them
the world was breaking again. And they
came to find the source.

**Verasanth is the source.**

### A.12.4 The Beautiful Irony

The Darmerians are feared as raiders.
Blamed for the Aridari conflict. Seen as
aggressors.

But in truth: they are the only people
who came to Verasanth actively trying to
find what is wrong with the world.

The Aridari reacted to the Darmerians.
The Darmerians reacted to the wound.

The conflict between them was
misunderstanding layered on fear, not
genuine enmity. Neither people was the
true enemy. Both were reacting to
the same invisible force.

### A.12.5 Their Relationship to the Silth

The Darmerians never asked for the Silth
to be created.

They did not know the Aridari were
building a response to their raids. They
did not know their coastal pressure was
being interpreted as an existential threat
to a people who were already watching
their world destabilize.

The Silth exist because the Darmerians
were frightening enough to break an
entire civilization's unity.

The Darmerians carry no guilt for this —
they were surviving, as they always had.

But the Silth know what they represent.
That tension never fully resolves.

### A.12.6 The Three Sub-types

Set during character creation if race === "darmerians".
Flag: dar_mark

These are expressions of how the Drowned
Coast shaped them — which part of the
sea's lesson they carry most deeply.

---

**The Tide-Marked**
*"We endure what takes everything else."*

These Darmerians are shaped by loss and
resilience. They carry the deepest
salt-crystal markings. They are the
emotional core of their people — the ones
who remember what was lost and refuse to
let it define them as broken.

Traits: Steady, grounded, emotionally
resilient, deeply protective. The anchors
of their kin-bands.

City reaction: Verasanth's instability
does not rattle them. They have survived
worse. They hold.

Flavor: *"You have already lost everything
once. You know how to stand after."*

Flag value: "tide_marked"

---

**The Storm-Called**
*"If the storm comes, we meet it."*

These Darmerians are shaped by fury and
momentum. Where others see danger, they
see the moment to move. Bold, loud,
charismatic, fearless. They are the
frontline thunder — the ones who charge
into the unknown because hesitation is
what killed their neighbors.

Traits: High energy, decisive, magnetic.
They inspire others by moving first.

City reaction: The city's chaos feels
like a storm they know how to read. They
are not calm in it. They are alive in it.

Flavor: *"The storm is here. Good.
You know what to do in storms."*

Flag value: "storm_called"

---

**The Deep-Sung**
*"The ocean remembers what we do not."*

These Darmerians are shaped by the sea's
depth and mystery. Not magical — but
attuned. Generations of listening to the
tides left something in their bloodline
that reads the world's subtle signals.
Quiet, perceptive, uncanny. They navigate
by instinct others can't name.

Traits: Reflective, observant, spiritually
attuned in a way that feels earned not
granted. They are the navigators — of
water, of people, of fate.

City reaction: Verasanth feels like the
sea before a storm. They know how to read
it. They just don't know yet what it's
warning them about.

Flavor: *"You hear something in the stone
that sounds like water. You know what
that means. You just don't know yet
what to do about it."*

Flag value: "deep_sung"

### A.12.8 Relationship to Other Races

**With all Aridari:**
Complicated. They were once the threat
that broke a people's unity. Now they
see the Aridari as fellow survivors of
the same invisible catastrophe.
*"We were never your enemy."*

**With Silth:**
Tension that has no clean resolution.
The Silth were made because of them.
The Darmerians never asked for that.
*"You were built because of us. We
never asked you to be."*

**With Cambral:**
Mutual respect between builders.
One builds with stone, one with ships.
Both understand what it means to give
form to something that must hold.

**With Mal'Aridari:**
Deep kinship. Both are communal,
empathetic, shaped by loss.
*"You heal. We endure. Between us,
nothing stays broken long."*

**With Pan'Aridari:**
Admiration.
*"You follow flow. We ride storms.
We understand each other."*

**With Dak'Aridari:**
Mutual wariness born of mutual respect.
Both are shaped by survival in hostile
environments that would kill others.

**With Ashborn:**
Volatile admiration.
*"You burn. We crash. Together we are
very loud and very dangerous."*

### A.12.9 Cultural Identity

Three core principles:

1. **Courage grows stronger when shared.**
   Bravery is not individual. It is
   contagious.

2. **The storm does not negotiate.**
   Neither do you. But you survive it.

3. **What the sea took, you remember.**
   Loss is not weakness. It is history
   you carry forward.

Cultural behaviors:
- Build longships even in the city,
  though there is no sea to sail
- Hold feasts that double as strategy
  councils
- Tattoo their bodies with the stories
  of storms survived
- Speak loudly — silence is what the
  drowned villages sounded like
- Never leave a kin-band member behind

How others see them:
- Loud
- Fierce
- Warm once trusted
- Unpredictable until known
- *"They look at you like they're
  deciding whether you'd survive
  a storm."*

### A.12.11 Design Rules for Darmerian Content

- Their ferocity is always defiance,
  never cruelty — make this felt
- The Silth tension is never resolved
  cleanly — both sides have valid
  grievances
- Seris knows the coastal rupture
  connection — she does not say so
  immediately
- Gautrorn's sea memories and Darmerian
  players share a resonance — they
  recognize each other's references
- Never make them simple raiders —
  they are the world's warning system
- The drowned coast is never shown,
  only remembered
- Deep-Sung Darmerians and
  Echo-Sensed Dak'Aridari have an
  uncanny recognition of each other

🔥 Core Darmerian Identity (Locked)

Let’s define this cleanly:

They are NOT:
conquerors
empire-builders
mindless brutes
They ARE:
battle-driven
honor-bound (their version of it)
competitive even among themselves
a culture that measures worth through conflict
🧬 Visual Direction — Finalized

Now we refine their look to match THIS version.

🐂 1. Silhouette: Built for Combat, Not Labor
Broad upper body (shoulders > hips)
Forward-leaning posture — like they’re always ready to engage
Weight carried through the front of the body (not upright and regal like knights)

👉 From a distance:

They look like they expect a fight at any moment

🪨 2. Bone Structure (Your “not-minotaur” — now perfected)

We keep the horn implication, but tie it to combat evolution, not animal mimicry:

Thick brow ridges (protective, like natural armor)
Subtle outward sweep at temples (suggests horn growth, never fully formed)
Reinforced jawlines and cheekbones (impact-resistant look)

👉 Result:

Faces that look like they’ve been hit before — and kept going

⚡ 3. Skin & Markings = History of Conflict

Not just “cool patterns” — these are earned surfaces

Scar layering is common — not hidden, not ornamental
Salt-crystal or mineral deposits still work, but now feel like:
coastal origin
hardened exposure
Skin tones may have weathered, uneven texture — like someone who lives outside comfort

Optional variation:

Ritual scarring or carved markings to track victories or survival

👉 Distinctive:

You don’t wonder if they’ve fought. You know they have.

🔥 4. Movement = Controlled Aggression

This is HUGE for their identity:

Stillness that feels like a held strike
Quick, decisive bursts of motion
No wasted energy — but also no hesitation

👉 Difference from others:

Cambral = deliberate
Dak’Aridari = cautious
Pan’Aridari = fluid
Darmerians = decisive

They don’t wait to understand the fight.
They step into it.

👁️ 5. Eyes = Competitive Fire (not rage)

This is where you avoid cliché:

Not berserker rage
Not constant anger

Instead:

Intensity
Focus
Appraisal

👉 Expression:

“Are you worth fighting?”

Not hostility.
Evaluation.

⚔️ 6. Cultural Detail (this is GOLD for immersion)

Because they fight each other too:

Mixed armor styles (taken, traded, won)
Weapons that look used, not ceremonial
Trophy elements — but not barbaric excess

👉 Key visual:

Nothing about them looks untouched by conflict

⚖️ The Most Important Balance (don’t break this)

You NEED to preserve this tension:

They caused fear great enough to create the Silth…
but they were never trying to conquer the world.

So visually:

❌ Don’t make them look evil
❌ Don’t make them look monstrous
❌ Don’t make them look chaotic idiots
✅ Make them look like:

People who will absolutely fight you…
and genuinely respect you if you survive it

🧠 How This Reflects the Aridari Schism (beautifully)

Now this becomes visible:

Aridari Reaction	What they saw
Mal → “This is wrong”	Endless conflict
Pan → “This is dangerous”	Unpredictable aggression
Dak → “This is survival”	A real, physical threat
Coalition → “We need something stronger”	Darmerians

👉 And the player will feel that just by looking at them.

🔥 Final Darmerian One-Line Identity

This is your anchor:

Battle-forged, forward-built figures marked by a lifetime of conflict — they don’t seek to rule the world, only to prove themselves against it.
