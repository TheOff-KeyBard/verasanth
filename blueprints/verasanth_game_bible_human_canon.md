## NEW §A.13 — Humans: Full Canon

### A.13.1 Origin

Humans were not dragged here.
They were not called here.
They were not shaped, created, or displaced.

They found the cracks — and walked through.

They are the Threshold Host — the only race
in Verasanth defined not by what happened
to them, but by what they chose to do.

### A.13.2 How They Found Verasanth

Across the world, things were wrong in ways
that didn't add up.

- Ruins that didn't match any known history
- Caves that led somewhere they shouldn't
- Mirrors that reflected different places
- Storms that didn't behave naturally
- Coastlines that felt thinner than they should

Other races felt these wrongnesses and
responded according to their nature —
the Mal'Aridari followed the wound, the
Darmerians followed the pressure, the
Cambral followed the resonance.

Humans noticed the wrongness and did
something different.

They investigated.

A scholar steps through a ruin.
A scavenger follows a tunnel too far.
A sailor enters a wrong fog bank.
A pilgrim finds a shrine that shouldn't exist.

They all end up in the same place.

**Verasanth doesn't call humans.
It leaves doors open — and humans
walk through them.**

### A.13.3 What Drives Them

Not wealth. Not power. These are surface
motivations, and Verasanth burns through
surface motivations quickly.

The real drivers:

- Curiosity
- Defiance
- Restlessness
- Refusal to accept limits
- The need to understand
- The belief that something is just
  beyond reach

Humans don't need a reason to go somewhere
they shouldn't. They need a reason not to —
and rarely listen to it.

### A.13.4 Their Place in the World System

Every other race is bound to something:

| Race | Bound To |
|------|----------|
| Ashborn | Fire |
| Dak'Aridari | Depth |
| Pan'Aridari | Flow |
| Mal'Aridari | Life |
| Cambral | Structure |
| Silth | Purpose (created) |
| Darmerians | Consequence |
| Humans | Choice |

This is the cleanest identity in the system.
Every other race has a relationship to a
force or a trauma that precedes them.
Humans have agency. Nothing else.

### A.13.5 Why They Matter in Verasanth

Every other race asks:
*"What is this place?"*
*"Why are we here?"*
*"How do we survive it?"*

Humans ask:
*"What can I do with this?"*

That makes them unpredictable, adaptable,
dangerous, and essential. They are the
variable in a system of patterns. The
wildcard every system eventually produces.

The city does not know what to do with them.
The Ledger records them and moves on.
The demon finds them irrelevant — until
a human does something that changes
the equation.

### A.13.6 No Sub-types — Design Rule

**Humans have no character creation sub-type.**

This is intentional and load-bearing.

Every other race has defined paths because
something defined those paths for them —
a force, a trauma, a collapse, a creation
event. The sub-type reflects how they
adapted to what was done to them.

Humans were not done to. They arrived.

If sub-types were added, it would imply
humans are shaped by something. They are
not. They are defined by instinct choice,
decisions made in play, and playstyle.

**Humans are what the player makes them.
That is their mechanic.**

### A.13.7 In-Game Flavor Text

**Ledger interactions:**

*"The Ledger records you. Origin: threshold
crossing, voluntary. Classification: human.
Note: subject entered without compulsion.
The Ledger does not have a category for this.
It creates one."*

**Seris first meeting (humans only):**
*"She looks at you for a moment.
Then: 'You came through on your own.'
It is not a question.
Something about it amuses her —
briefly, and then it doesn't."*

**Environmental flavor:**
*"You shouldn't be here. You know that.
You came anyway. That feels about right."*

### A.13.8 How Others See Humans

- *"They don't belong to anything."*
- *"They change too quickly."*
- *"They don't respect boundaries."*
- *"They survive things they shouldn't."*

Other races are defined. Humans are
unsettling precisely because they aren't.

### A.13.9 Relationship to Other Races

**With Aridari (all branches):**
*"You broke yourselves over principles.
We just walked in."*

**With Silth:**
*"You were made for a purpose.
We make our own."*

**With Cambral:**
*"You build. We use what you built."*

**With Mal'Aridari:**
*"You heal. We ask whether it should
be healed."*

**With Pan'Aridari:**
*"You follow the flow. We disrupt it."*

**With Dak'Aridari:**
*"You survive the depths.
We go down anyway."*

**With Darmerians:**
*"You endured the storm.
We walked into it on purpose."*

**With Ashborn:**
*"You were changed by the city.
We're still deciding if we want to be."*

### A.13.10 Cultural Identity

Humans have no unified culture in Verasanth.
They arrived individually, through different
cracks, at different times, for different
reasons.

What they share is not culture.
It is disposition.

- They ask questions others stopped asking
- They test limits others stopped testing
- They form alliances based on usefulness,
  not kinship
- They adapt faster than any other race —
  not because they are better, but because
  they have no fixed shape to return to

### A.13.11 Player-Facing Description (Website Canon)

"Adaptable and unpredictable, shaped by
choice rather than lineage.

In a city that reshapes everyone who enters
it, humans are the ones who walked in
without being pushed.

They are not here because something brought
them. They are here because they found a
door that shouldn't exist — and opened it.

Every other race in Verasanth was defined
by something that happened to them.

Humans are still deciding what defines them.

That makes them the most dangerous thing
in the city."

### A.13.12 Design Rules for Human Content

- Never give humans a cosmic origin or
  connection to a force — they have none
- Their defining trait is voluntary entry —
  always reference this in flavor text
- The Ledger's confusion about them is
  brief — it categorizes them and moves on.
  That dismissal is itself meaningful.
- Seris finds them briefly amusing —
  she has never met a race that arrived
  on purpose before
- Humans should occasionally get flavor
  text that other races don't — not because
  they're special, but because they notice
  different things
- Never let them feel like "default" —
  in Verasanth, voluntary entry is the
  most unusual thing a person can do
- No sub-types. Ever. This is permanent.

🧍 HUMANS — FINAL VISUAL IDENTITY (Player-Facing Canon)
Threshold Host — Those Defined Only by What They Choose

🧬 1. Silhouette — No Pattern, No Anchor
Humans have no shared silhouette.
- Tall, short, broad, lean — all valid
- Graceful or awkward
- Strong or slight
- No consistent posture
- No instinctive movement pattern
From a distance:
They look like anyone.
And that means they could become anything.
Every other race is shaped by something.
Humans are shaped by choice.

⚖️ 2. Presence — The Absence That Becomes Identity
Every race carries a signature presence:
- Mal’Aridari → calm
- Pan’Aridari → motion
- Dak’Aridari → depth
- Cambral → weight
- Ashborn → tension
- Silth → purpose
- Darmerians → conflict
Humans?
Nothing consistent.
And that absence is what people notice.
They don’t feel anchored to anything.
They feel unclaimed.

👁️ 3. Eyes — The Unpredictable Element
Human eyes follow no pattern.
- Any color
- Any intensity
- Any emotional baseline
But what sets them apart is this:
Their eyes don’t reflect what they are.
They reflect what they decide.
NPC reaction:
“I can’t tell what they’re going to do.”

🧠 4. Expression & Behavior — Mutable, Volatile, Chosen
Humans are the only race whose:
- posture changes with mood
- movement changes with intention
- identity shifts over time
They can:
- look confident and hesitate
- look unsure and act decisively
- appear harmless and become dangerous
There is no visual consistency.
And that is the consistency.

🧬 5. Skin, Hair, Features — Total Variation
Humans have:
- all skin tones
- all hair textures
- all facial structures
- all body types
No biological tell.
No shared marker.
No inherited signature.
Nothing about them explains them.

⚔️ 6. Movement — Chosen, Not Given
Every other race moves according to something:
- instinct
- adaptation
- design
- environment
- history
Humans?
They move how they choose to.
- Hesitant or decisive
- Careful or reckless
- Predictable or chaotic
And it can change moment to moment.

🧍 7. Distinctive Trait (Across a Room)
You don’t recognize a human by what they are.
You recognize them by what they do next.
- They step where others wouldn’t
- They ask questions others stopped asking
- They take risks without understanding them
Then you realize:
They didn’t have to be here.
They chose to be.

🧬 8. Optional Variation — Maximum Player Identity Space
Humans are the only race where:
Everything is player-defined.
- appearance
- posture
- expression
- behavior
- emotional presence
Because their identity is not given at creation.
It is built through action.

👁️ 9. How NPCs Read Them — The Unsettling Freedom
Other races don’t categorize humans easily.
They think:
- “They don’t belong to anything.”
- “They change too quickly.”
- “They don’t respect boundaries.”
- “They might do something no one else would.”
Humans are unpredictable not because they’re chaotic —
but because they’re unbound.

⚠️ 10. The Unsettling Truth — The Ledger’s Dismissal
Your canon nails this:
The Ledger records them… and moves on.
That should feel:
- dismissive
- bureaucratic
- slightly insulting
- and quietly dangerous
Because systems don’t fear humans.
Systems underestimate them.
And that’s why humans break systems.
