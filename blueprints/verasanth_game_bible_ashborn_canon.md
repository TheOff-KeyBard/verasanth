## NEW §A.6 — The Ashborn: Full Canon

### A.6.1 Origin

The Ashborn predate the Ledger. They are
Verasanth's first children — the First Host.

Before the city was Verasanth. Before the Ledger
stirred. Before the demon began its current hunts.
There was an original wave of captives: humans
dragged across the veil by a demon that had been
shackled to complete a city it did not build.

**The demon did not build Verasanth. It was forced
to complete it.** Something older — the same force
behind the Ledger — began constructing the city
before the demon arrived. The demon was compelled
to drag in humans to fill it. The First Host were
the earliest population seeds. The test subjects
for the Pale Fires. The catalyst for the city's
awakening.

### A.6.2 The Pale Fires

The Pale Fires were not natural flames. They were:
- Memory-eating
- Emotion-reactive
- Reality-softening
- Half-alive

Exposure did not kill the First Host. It rewrote
them. Their bodies adapted to survive:
- Ember-veins formed to vent emotional overload
- Senses sharpened to detect shifts in the city's mood
- Emotions became volatile because the Fires fed on intensity

They became the Ashborn, though they didn't call
themselves that yet. They were simply the ones
who endured.

### A.6.3 Why the Ledger Is Silent About Them

The Ledger's silence is not an omission. It is a scar.

The First Host predates the Ledger's awakening.
More accurately: **the First Host is what caused
the Ledger to awaken.**

When the Pale Fires rewrote human bodies, something
in the city reacted. A dormant mechanism — magical,
mechanical, divine, or alien — flickered to life.
The Ledger began recording.

But it could not record what came before its own birth.

The First Host exists in a blind spot:
- No entries
- No lineage markers
- No origin tags
- No cause or purpose documented

The Ashborn are the only race in Verasanth whose
existence the Ledger cannot fully account for.

### A.6.4 Why No One Remembers

Three reasons, all reinforcing each other:

1. **The Pale Fires burned their memories.**
   The earliest Ashborn lost huge chunks of identity,
   history, and purpose.

2. **The demon erased what remained.**
   Not out of malice — out of fear. The First Host
   saw something in the city's foundations that the
   demon didn't want remembered.

3. **The Ledger cannot record what predates its awakening.**
   So the past simply isn't there.

### A.6.5 The Ashborn Today

Even without history, modern Ashborn carry:
- A sense of déjà vu when near the Pale Fires
- A bone-deep fear of being "called back"
- Dreams of a burning city that isn't Verasanth
- A subconscious recognition of the demon
- A strange comfort in the city's darkest corners

They are haunted by a past they cannot name.

### A.6.6 The Big Reveal (Long Arc)

The Ashborn are the key to understanding what
Verasanth truly is. They are:
- The first victims
- The first survivors
- The first catalysts
- The first witnesses
- The first anomaly the Ledger ever encountered

The demon's renewed kidnappings are not random.
It is looking for someone. A descendant of the
First Host who still carries the original,
uncorrupted ember-mark.

**This is the spine of Arc 2.**

### A.6.7 The Three Marks

Modern Ashborn develop one of three ember-mark
expressions. Set during character creation via
flag: ashborn_mark

**The Tempered**
Control is not suppression — it is survival.
Ashborn who contain the ember-veins deliberately.
Often mistrusted by other Ashborn. Appear nearly
human to outsiders. The city watches them more
carefully because of it.
Flag value: "tempered"

**The Kindled**
Emotion moves through them like weather — fast,
visible, and honest. Drawn to the Pale Fires.
Passionate, intense, unpredictable. Others find
this alarming. The Kindled find their discomfort
informative.
Flag value: "kindled"

**The Hollowed**
Something burned through and didn't leave much
behind. They function. They observe. They feel
the city's heat without reacting to it.
Emotionally burned out. Extremely unsettling
presence. The city is quieter around them.
Flag value: "hollowed"

Applied when Ashborn players trigger Ledger events
or interact with Seris at high trust.

### A.6.9 NPC Archetypes Using The Three Marks

The Tempered, Kindled, and Hollowed are available
as NPC archetypes for future Ashborn NPCs.
They should never be explained by NPCs directly —
only demonstrated through behavior and reaction.

### A.6.10 Design Rules for Ashborn Content

- Never fully explain them early
- The Ledger's silence is always present — never resolved
- Seris knows more than she says — always
- Dream fragments are memory, not invention
- The ember-mark is biological, not magical
- The demon recognizes them before they recognize it
- "The city's first adaptation" is the canonical
  one-line description — use it consistently

### A.6.11 Player-Facing Description (Website Canon)

"Humans reshaped by Verasanth's ancient fires.
Ember-veins pulse faintly beneath their skin,
brightening with emotion. They aren't infernal —
they are the city's first adaptation.

The Pale Fires didn't kill the earliest arrivals.
They rewrote them. Their bodies learned to vent
emotional overload through ember-veins. Their
senses sharpened to feel the city's shifts. Their
presence affects rooms in ways they don't fully
control.

The Ledger has no record of their origin.
It began recording after they arrived.

Some believe the city still recognizes them.
They are probably right."

🔥 ASHBORN — FINAL VISUAL IDENTITY (Player-Facing Canon)
Fire Host — The City’s First Adaptation

🔥 1. Silhouette — Human… Until It Isn’t
Ashborn look human at first glance.
- Human proportions
- Human posture
- Human movement patterns
But something is always slightly off:
- A tension under the skin
- A warmth in the air
- A sense of emotional pressure
From across a room:
You mistake them for human — until you look twice.

🔥 2. Ember-Veins — The Signature of the Fire Host
This is the defining visual anchor.
Beneath their skin:
- Faint ember-veins branch and pulse
- Not constant — reactive
- Respond to emotion, stress, or proximity to the city’s influence
Colors:
- Dim orange
- Soft red
- Pale gold (rare)
Glow behavior:
- Subtle at rest
- Brightens during emotional spikes
- Flickers like memory, not flame
It is not fire.
It is fire’s echo.
Something remembering what the Pale Fires did to them.

👁️ 3. Eyes — Emotion Made Visible
Ashborn eyes betray them.
Base colors:
Human-like — brown, gray, hazel, green.
But when emotion rises:
- A faint internal glow
- Light flickers behind the iris
- Intensity becomes visible even when expression stays neutral
Their eyes reveal what they’re feeling…
even when they don’t want them to.
NPC reaction:
“You don’t look angry… but your eyes do.”

🔥 4. Skin Tone & Texture — Burned Through, Not Burned
Their skin carries subtle signs of transformation:
- A faint warmth beneath the surface
- Occasional heat-distortion shimmer during strong emotion
- Rare individuals show pale, branching burn-like patterns
- Ember-veins sometimes sit closer to the surface
They don’t look burned.
They look like something inside them once burned through.

🌫️ 5. Hair — Normal… With a Hint of Heat
Hair behaves mostly like human hair, but:
- Edges may appear slightly lighter
- Strands catch light with a warm undertone
- In emotional spikes: faint shimmer or tonal shift
Always subtle.
Always peripheral.
Never dramatic.

⚖️ 6. Movement — Controlled Instability
Ashborn move like humans… until something inside them reacts.
At rest:
Indistinguishable from humans.
Under stress:
- Tension builds visibly
- Movements sharpen
- Reactions become faster, more volatile
They don’t move differently because of training.
They move differently because something inside them stirs.

🔥 7. Distinctive Trait (Across a Room)
You notice it when:
- The air feels slightly warmer
- A moment becomes emotionally charged
- Tension rises without explanation
Then you see them.
And realize:
They didn’t cause it.
But something in them amplified it.

🧬 8. Optional Variation — Player Expression Within Identity
Players can lean into:
Tempered
- Minimal visible glow
- Controlled, contained presence
- Ember-veins faint unless provoked
Kindled
- Expressive ember-veins
- Visible emotional shifts
- Heat shimmer more common
Hollowed
- Dim or nearly absent glow
- Unsettling calm
- Eyes feel empty, not fiery
Variations in:
- Ember-vein intensity
- Emotional visibility
- How “human” they appear
But always:
The fire is there.
Even when it’s quiet.

👁️ 9. How NPCs Read Them — The Unfinished Transformation
NPCs don’t think:
- “Mage”
- “Warrior”
- “Fire user”
They think:
“Something happened to them… and it didn’t finish.”
Reactions:
- Unease (instability)
- Curiosity (mystery)
- Recognition (some swear they’ve seen this before)

⚠️ 10. The Unsettling Truth — The Ledger Cannot Record Them
Your canon gives them a unique visual tension:
The Ledger cannot fully account for them.
This should be felt:
- Their presence sometimes feels unaccounted for
- Light behaves slightly differently around them
- Emotional spikes affect the space, not just them
Feeling:
“They don’t fully belong to the system that tracks everything else.”
